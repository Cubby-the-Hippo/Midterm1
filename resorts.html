Ski resorts in the us

Where to stay

Aspen Snowmass, Colorado

Aspen Snowmass offers over 5,500 acres of skiable terrain across four mountains, suitable for skiers of all levels. It's known for its luxury vibe, with top-notch dining, après-ski, and shopping experiences. The picturesque town of Aspen adds to its charm with rich culture and nightlife.

Jackson Hole, Wyoming

Renowned for its challenging slopes, Jackson Hole is a favorite for expert skiers and thrill-seekers. With steep terrain and deep powder, it’s perfect for adventure. The resort is also near Yellowstone and Grand Teton National Parks, offering stunning scenery.

Park City, Utah

Park City is the largest ski resort in the U.S., offering diverse runs and easy access from Salt Lake City. It’s ideal for families and skiers of all skill levels, with plenty of lodging and dining options. The town of Park City is known for its charm and the annual Sundance Film Festival.

Vail, Colorado

Vail boasts one of the largest single-mountain ski resorts in the U.S., with over 5,000 acres of varied terrain. It’s famous for its expansive back bowls and vibrant village atmosphere. With excellent snow conditions and luxurious amenities, it attracts skiers worldwide.

Breckenridge, Colorado

Known for its high elevation and historic mining town, Breckenridge offers a mix of terrain for beginners to experts. The resort’s five peaks provide expansive skiing options and breathtaking mountain views. It's a laid-back resort with a lively après-ski scene.
