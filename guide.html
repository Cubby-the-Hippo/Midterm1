Guide to Skiing: signs and safety

Signs and what they mean:

Beginer run sign:

The beginner ski sign is typically a green circle, which marks trails or slopes that are the easiest and safest for beginner skiers. These slopes are usually gentle, with a lower incline, making them ideal for people who are just learning to ski or want to practice basic skills.

Intermediate run sign:

The intermediate ski sign is usually marked by a blue square. This symbol indicates trails or slopes that are moderately difficult, meant for skiers who have moved beyond the beginner stage and are comfortable with slightly steeper terrain, more turns, and varying snow conditions.

Advanced run sign:

Advanced signs on ski slopes are marked with a black diamond. These slopes indicate a high level of difficulty with steep terrain, narrow paths, or challenging conditions like moguls. They're meant for experienced skiers who can handle more technical runs.

Expert ski runs:

Expert slopes are marked with double black diamonds. These runs are the most difficult on the mountain, featuring very steep terrain, narrow passages, and potential obstacles like rocks or cliffs. They're designed for highly skilled skiers who are comfortable navigating extreme conditions.
